## French Market Insights

### Tone & Formality
- Understated confidence over enthusiasm. Lead with evidence, not energy.
- No superlatives ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "#1") - French professionals find these unsophisticated.
- No artificial urgency ("window is closing," "competitors are moving now") - seen as manipulation.

### Structure & Cadence
- 80-150 words per email
- Logical progression: observation - problem - proof - soft CTA
- **5 email touches, spaced 4-5 days apart**
- **3 LinkedIn DMs** (DM 1 substantive, DMs 2-3 soft)
- **Total sequence: ~21 days**
- Each follow-up must add genuinely new value
- **Email 5 is always the ABC close** - prospect self-selects: interested / later / not relevant

### Subject Lines
- Under 40 characters
- Company-specific, direct, relevant

### CTAs
- Soft, permission-based ("Would it be useful?" / "Is this relevant?")
- No booking links until after reply

### Cultural Red Flags
- No credential dumping (15,000 teams, 7 years, ISO 27001 in one paragraph = bragging)
- No overselling - one proof point beats five statistics
- Boost.space appears in Email 1 as a one-sentence credibility line with Decathlon reference - no credential dump

## Revised Copy Rules

### Rules Carried Forward (unchanged)
1. Revenue over cost savings
2. Permission-based CTA - "Want to see it?" / "Is this useful?"
3. No booking link in messages
4. First person "I" - never "we" or "our team"
5. Company name in subjects, not {FirstName}
6. LinkedIn connection requests are empty
7. Top 20 research framing
8. Short emails - under 130 words ideally, 150 max
9. One idea per email
10. No em dashes - use hyphens only

### Rules Modified for France
11. **Spacing: 4-5 days** between emails (was 3)
12. **Total emails: 5** - each must add genuinely new value
13. **LinkedIn DMs: 3** - DM 1 substantive, DMs 2-3 soft
14. **Total sequence duration: ~21 days** (was 13)
15. **Email 5: ABC close format** - "A: interested, no time / B: get back to me later / C: not relevant"
16. **Decathlon: always in Email 1** as a one-sentence credibility anchor alongside Boost.space.
17. **Boost.space: one sentence in Email 1** with Decathlon reference. No credential dump. Can appear again briefly in Email 3 or 4.
18. **No credential dump.** One specific proof point per sequence max.
19. **No artificial urgency.** Let competitive data create urgency naturally.
20. **One concrete finding in Email 1.** Or a self-serve test they can run in 60 seconds.

### Behavioral Nudge Rules (NEW in v4.1)
21. **Email 1: full signature ({SenderName}).** First impression = credibility. No mobile sig.
22. **Emails 2-5: first name only ({SenderFirstName}).** Shifts tone to "we're in a conversation."
23. **Emails 2-5: mobile signature.** `Envoyé depuis mon iPad` - signals personal message, justifies brevity, kills mass-email perception.
24. **Emails 2 and 4: PS line.** Highest-read part of email. Second hook without adding body length. Must be a concrete data point or self-serve test.
25. **Emails 3-4: "just reply X" friction reducer.** One-word reply is psychologically easier than composing a sentence.
26. **Email 5: shortened ABC options.** Punchier, less formal - matches the conversational tone built through the sequence.
27. **LinkedIn DMs: no nudges.** LinkedIn is inherently informal - nudges would be redundant.
